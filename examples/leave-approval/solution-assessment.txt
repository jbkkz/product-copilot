DESIGN DECISIONS
  ✓ Approval circuit is configurable per client/country/contract rather than a
    single fixed workflow
      Why          Business rules for leave approval already differ across
                   clients and countries in the current process.
      Alternative  A single standardized workflow for all clients.
      Tradeoff     Higher build and configuration complexity now, in exchange
                   for avoiding custom forks per client later.
  ✓ Balance/quota is revalidated at approval time, not only at submission
      Why          Overlapping requests are allowed, so a balance check only at
                   submission could approve requests that later overdraw the
                   balance.
      Alternative  Check balance once at submission and trust it through
                   approval.
      Tradeoff     Extra revalidation logic and concurrency handling, in
                   exchange for balances that stay accurate under overlapping
                   requests.
  ✓ Calendar sync is one-way (system to calendar), with no payroll integration
    in this phase
  ✓ HR is the sole editor of approved requests and the sole validator of
    cancellations
  Still to decide
    • Whether escalation timing is configurable per client/country or fixed at 5
      business days
    • How balance is restored when already-started leave is cancelled, and how
      that reconciles with payroll
    • Scope of historical data/balance migration before go-live
    • Delivery timeline and formal success metrics
    • Reporting and audit-of-changes requirements, including visibility into
      entitlement rule changes

CHALLENGES
  ⚑ Fixed 5-day escalation, but rules vary by client
      Premise      Auto-escalation always fires after exactly 5 business days.
      Alternative  Since approval circuits and entitlement rules already vary by
                   client, country and contract, the escalation window likely
                   needs to vary too (e.g. shorter for urgent contractor
                   missions, longer where HR reviews are batched weekly).
      Consequence  A hardcoded window will need a follow-up change request the
                   first time a client asks for something different, and by then
                   it's wired into the scheduler and notification logic.
      Recommend    Make escalation timing a per-client/country config value from
                   day one, even if the pilot ships with a single default.
  ⚑ Cancellation of started leave and balance restoration
      Premise      HR can validate cancellation of already-started leave,
                   implicitly restoring the balance.
      Alternative  Without payroll integration, the system's balance and the
                   payroll system's deductions can silently diverge once a
                   started leave is cancelled retroactively.
      Consequence  Employees see a 'corrected' balance in the tool that doesn't
                   match their payslip, undermining the exact trust this project
                   is meant to build.
      Recommend    Define the balance-restoration rule for started-leave
                   cancellations explicitly with HR/Finance, and flag the
                   payroll gap as a known limitation of this phase.
  ⚑ No migration of historical leave data
      Premise      The new system starts clean, without porting historical
                   requests or balances.
      Alternative  French RTT/paid-leave balances often carry over year to year;
                   starting from an unverified 'day-one' balance risks disputes
                   exactly like the ones this project is meant to eliminate.
      Consequence  Audit trail credibility is weakened right at launch if an
                   employee's opening balance can't be traced back to anything,
                   and HR ends up reconciling by hand anyway.
      Recommend    At minimum, migrate current balance snapshots (not full
                   history) so the audit trail has a defensible starting point.

    · delegation logic layered on top of an already shared permission framework

  Cost driver   the per-client/country configurable approval and entitlement
                rule engine with concurrency-safe balance checks

MAIN RISKS
  ⚠ Approval and permission logic are shared platform components — changes here
    can ripple into contracts, invoicing and other modules that reuse the same
    framework
  ⚠ Escalation timers racing against manager/delegate action could produce
    duplicate approvals or approvals firing after escalation has already
    occurred
  ⚠ Skipping historical data migration weakens the audit trail exactly where
    French RTT carryover rules make balance provenance sensitive
  ⚠ Notification routing by contact role will break silently if external client
    contacts don't have roles properly set
  ⚠ GDPR handling of HR data (leave reasons, balances) needs explicit scoping
    given the new audit and reporting surface

OPPORTUNITIES
  High leverage
    ◆ Generalize the approval-circuit engine so other workflows (mission
      validation, invoice sign-off) can reuse the same configurable multi-level
      logic.
  Future idea
    ◆ Turn the entitlement rule template surface into a shared 'business rule
      configuration' module usable beyond leave (contracts, invoicing rules).
    ◆ Extract the delegation layer as a cross-module capability (out-of-office
      delegation for any approval, not just leave).
