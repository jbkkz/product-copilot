# Digital Leave Approval Workflow

> Product Requirements Document — generated by Product Copilot

## Summary

This project replaces the current email-and-spreadsheet process for requesting and approving employee leave with a configurable, auditable digital workflow. It enforces real-time balance checks, routes approvals through a multi-level circuit with automatic escalation, and gives HR full oversight and control over exceptions and cancellations. The solution is designed for a pilot rollout with one client before being scaled to others with different rules.

## Problem

Leave approval today happens over email and Excel, with no systematic balance/quota check and no reliable audit trail. This leads to inconsistent approvals, errors in leave balances, and no clear record of who approved what and when.

## Goals

- Reduce leave balance errors through automated, real-time quota checks
- Provide a full audit trail of every request, approval, rejection, escalation and cancellation
- Ensure approvals and escalations happen within a predictable timeframe
- Successfully validate the approach with a pilot client before wider rollout

## Users & roles

- Employee (submits leave requests, sees own balance and status)
- Manager (first-level approver)
- HR (second-level approver above threshold, sole editor of approved requests, validates cancellations)
- Administrator (configures approval circuits and entitlement rules per client)
- External client contacts (relevant to notification routing where applicable)

## Scope

**In scope**

- Leave request submission, approval and rejection workflow
- Real-time balance/quota check before approval
- Configurable multi-level approval circuit (manager, then HR above a threshold)
- Auto-escalation after a defined period of inactivity
- Delegation of approval authority
- HR-validated cancellation, including cancellation of already-started leave
- Per-client configuration of approval circuits and entitlement rules (country/contract variations)
- Email and in-app notifications on key workflow events
- One-way sync of approved leave to an external calendar
- Multi-tenant configuration isolation to support a pilot-then-scale rollout

**Out of scope**

- Payroll integration
- Two-way calendar sync
- Reporting/dashboard features beyond basic audit visibility
- Migration of historical leave requests into the new audit trail

## Workflow

1. Employee submits a leave request
2. System checks balance/quota; request is rejected outright if insufficient
3. Request routes to manager for approval
4. If above threshold, request additionally routes to HR after manager approval
5. If no action within 5 business days, request auto-escalates to the next approver or delegate
6. Request is marked approved or rejected, with notification sent to the employee
7. Approved leave syncs to the external calendar
8. If cancellation is needed (including after leave has started), HR reviews and validates the cancellation separately from the original approval flow

## Business rules

- A balance/quota check must pass before a request can be approved
- Requests above a configurable threshold require HR approval in addition to manager approval
- Approval circuit and entitlement rules vary by client, country and contract
- HR is the sole role permitted to edit an already-approved request
- Cancellation of leave, including already-started leave, requires HR validation and follows a separate flow from initial approval
- Auto-escalation triggers after 5 business days of approver inactivity

## Permissions

- Employee: submit requests, view own balance and request status
- Manager: approve/reject first-level requests for their team
- HR: approve requests above threshold, edit approved requests, validate cancellations
- Administrator: configure approval circuits and entitlement rules per client
- Delegation: a designated delegate temporarily assumes an approver's permissions for pending requests

## Integrations & notifications

- Email notifications on submission, approval, rejection, escalation and cancellation
- In-app notifications on the same key events
- One-way sync of approved leave to an external calendar system

## Edge cases

- Overlapping leave requests are allowed at submission but revalidated for conflicts/balance at approval time
- Cancellation of leave that has already started, requiring HR validation
- Race condition between an approver acting and the auto-escalation timer firing at the same time
- Concurrent approval/balance updates on overlapping requests need to be handled without double-counting or corrupting balances

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] An employee can submit a request and see it move through manager and, where applicable, HR approval
- [ ] A request with insufficient balance cannot be approved
- [ ] A request left unactioned for 5 business days auto-escalates to the correct next approver or delegate
- [ ] An approved request can only be edited by HR
- [ ] A started leave can be cancelled only through HR validation, and the cancellation is recorded in the audit trail
- [ ] Notifications are sent for submission, approval, rejection, escalation and cancellation
- [ ] Approval circuit and threshold rules can be configured differently for at least two clients without cross-impact
- [ ] All state changes are visible in an audit trail with actor and timestamp

## Assumptions

- French labour law rules (paid leave, RTT) are implemented as one of several configurable country templates
- No payroll integration is required at this stage
- Calendar sync is one-way (system to calendar) only
- Historical leave requests will not be migrated into the new system's audit trail

## Risks

- Approval circuits and permissions are shared across other platform modules, so changes here may ripple into unrelated screens or workflows
- Concurrent updates to leave balances could cause race conditions if not handled carefully
- Not migrating historical requests weakens the completeness of the audit trail from day one
- Undefined delivery timeline could affect pilot planning and stakeholder expectations
- Complex per-client configurability increases testing surface and risk of misconfiguration during rollout
